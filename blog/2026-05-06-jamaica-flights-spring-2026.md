## What it is

Round-trip flights from Fort Lauderdale (FLL) to Montego Bay, Jamaica (MBJ) are currently bookable starting around $235–$254 for travel through late May and early June. Typical pricing on this route runs $400–$420 roundtrip. That's a 40–43% drop from what you'd normally pay.

JetBlue is the main carrier on this route and has been aggressive all spring. Other airlines operating FLL–MBJ include Spirit (now operating under new management post-bankruptcy) and some American codeshares. Check Google Flights first — sort by price and filter for nonstop, which is available on this route.

This isn't an isolated Jamaica story. Dollar Flight Club's spring 2026 pricing analysis found Caribbean fares broadly suppressed, with Montego Bay, Aruba, and Puerto Rico all showing consistent sub-$250 roundtrips from South Florida throughout the March–May window. Jamaica has been one of the strongest value plays of the group.

## Why it's worth it

At $400+ a typical ticket, Jamaica has historically been priced out of reach for a spontaneous trip. At $235, it isn't. For a 4–5 night itinerary with a reasonable hotel in Negril or Montego Bay, total trip cost becomes genuinely competitive with domestic alternatives.

The 40% discount also compounds with Jamaica's own affordability. Budget-friendly guesthouses in Negril run $75–$120/night. Montego Bay has plenty of all-inclusive options. You're not saving on flights just to blow it on a $500/night resort unless you want to.

For context: this is one of the deeper spring discounts on a Caribbean route that Dollar Flight Club has documented in 2026. The CEO called out the East Coast and South Florida Caribbean pricing specifically as "one of the better booking windows since pre-pandemic pricing normalized."

## How to book it

Search Google Flights for FLL to MBJ. The best prices appear on nonstop JetBlue flights departing Tuesday or Wednesday — midweek departures are running 15–25% cheaper than weekend flights on this route. Aim for May 12–June 8 travel dates. Late May (around Memorial Day, May 25) is a blackout zone where prices will start climbing — build in a few days of buffer.

Book direct on JetBlue's site once you've confirmed the fare on Google Flights. You'll need to verify the fare class — Blue Basic fares are the cheapest but come with a few restrictions (see below).

## The catch

Two things to know before booking:

**Fare class.** JetBlue's Blue Basic tier — where the cheapest fares live — doesn't allow free seat selection and charges a $75–$100 fee for changes. It's not non-refundable per se, but it's also not flexible. If your plans are firm, it's fine. If they're not, spend $20–30 more for the next fare class up.

**Airport.** This pricing is specific to FLL (Fort Lauderdale). Fares from Miami (MIA), Orlando (MCO), or other Florida airports are meaningfully higher — in the $320–$380 range. If you're not in South Florida, your origin airport matters. Check your home airport separately; some East Coast hubs (Philadelphia, Newark, Detroit) are also showing sub-$250 fares to Puerto Rico and Jamaica, but verify before assuming.

## How long it'll last

This is a spring pricing trend, not a flash sale. There's no code, no countdown timer, no specific airline promotion. Prices are lower because demand is structurally softer for Caribbean travel between spring break and summer peak.

That window closes around Memorial Day — May 25, 2026. Airlines historically shift to summer pricing in the final 10–14 days before the holiday weekend. If you're going to book this, do it in the next 7–10 days to lock in fares before the shift. Don't wait until the week of Memorial Day expecting these prices to still be there.

Travel dates through early-to-mid June can still be cheap if booked now. If you're looking at late June or July, these prices won't apply — budget accordingly.
