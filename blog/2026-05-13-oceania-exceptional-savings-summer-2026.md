## What it is

Oceania Cruises is running its "Exceptional Savings" promotion through June 30, 2026. Select sailings across Europe, Alaska, the Caribbean, and Asia are discounted up to 40% off standard veranda stateroom pricing — veranda cabins that typically start above $4,000 per person on 10-night voyages are currently advertised at around $238 per night, or roughly $2,380 for a 10-night sailing. On top of that: complimentary Wi-Fi, a choice of amenity (free shore excursions or a beverage package), up to $1,000 in onboard ship credit per stateroom, and a $45 gift card.

The deal covers a wide range of 2026 sailings: Mediterranean and Northern Europe itineraries, Alaska voyages, Caribbean sailings, and select Asia routes.

## Why it's worth it

Oceania competes in the premium segment — ships that carry 600–1,200 passengers, a genuine focus on food (they run culinary-focused itineraries and have multiple specialty restaurants included), and a noticeably quieter atmosphere than the mega-ship lines. If that's the product you want, 35–40% off is a real number. At $2,000–$3,000 per person for a 10–14 night European sailing with meals included, you're reaching a price point that's competitive with large-ship premium options while the experience is meaningfully different.

The $1,000 ship credit adds up fast. Shore excursions on Oceania run $80–$200+ per person. Cover a couple of those and you've spent the credit on something you'd have paid for anyway. If excursions aren't your thing, it spends on spa, specialty wine, or their Canyon Ranch wellness program.

For context: this is not Oceania's typical discounting behavior. They normally shave 10–20% on specific slow-selling sailings. A sitewide 40%-off window with a named promotional period is a different posture — likely responding to softened demand in the premium cruise segment after a complicated 2026 travel market.

The refundable deposit is the part worth emphasizing. Most cruise sale rates are non-refundable — you lock in the price but you're committed. This promotion is explicitly only available on bookings with a refundable deposit. That means you can hold a sailing at today's discounted price and still cancel before the penalty window kicks in if plans change. For a large purchase that depends on factors like work schedules or travel partners, that matters.

## The catch

**"Select sailings, select categories" is real.** Availability is capacity-controlled, which means the discounted inventory thins out as popular sailings fill. A late August Norway cruise or a September Amalfi itinerary — those sell. If you have a specific sailing in mind, check it before assuming the discount applies. The deal may show 40% off on one departure and nothing on the adjacent week.

**Airfare is on you, and it's not small.** Oceania ships depart from ports like Barcelona, Venice, Lisbon, Istanbul, Vancouver, and Anchorage. Round-trip economy airfare from the US to those cities runs $600–$1,200+ per person right now — more in summer given current transatlantic pricing. The cruise discount is real, but it doesn't cover getting there. Build it into the total before the number starts looking too good.

**This is still a premium product.** Even at 40% off, most sailings run $2,000–$4,000+ per person for the cruise alone. That's not the same comparison as a discounted flight or hotel night. Know what you're evaluating.

## Where to book and when it expires

Book directly at **oceaniacruises.com/special-offers/savings** or call 855-OCEANIA. The promotion runs through **June 30, 2026**.

To access the sale, you need to select the refundable deposit option at booking — that's the condition that unlocks the Exceptional Savings pricing. It's a checkbox in the booking flow, not a separate promo code.

Don't assume availability holds until the end of June. Mediterranean summer sailings tend to sell through in the spring. When the discounted cabin inventory sells out on a given voyage, the promotion stops applying to that sailing. If a specific itinerary is on your shortlist, check it this week rather than next month.
