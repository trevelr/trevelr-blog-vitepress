## What it is

Princess Cruises is listing a 7-night Mediterranean itinerary through Travelzoo at $899 per person for interior cabins — that's $740 off the standard direct-booking price of roughly $1,639. Balcony cabins start at $1,089. The sailing visits five ports:

- **Corfu, Greece** — Ionian island with a Venetian Old Town and manageable crowds compared to Santorini
- **Dubrovnik, Croatia** — still worth it despite the fame; best seen before the day-trippers arrive in the morning
- **Kotor, Montenegro** — walled medieval city at the end of a fjord-like bay; the most underrated stop on any Adriatic itinerary
- **Naples, Italy** — gateway to Pompeii, Herculaneum, and Vesuvius
- **Civitavecchia (Rome), Italy** — 90 minutes by train from Termini Station

These are spring and fall 2026 sailings. Travelzoo has the deal listed for both seasons.

## Why it's worth it

$899 for seven nights on a Princess ship — dining and basic onboard activities included — works out to $128 per night. That's cheaper than a budget hotel in Dubrovnik or Rome for a week, and you're waking up in a different port every day or two.

The itinerary is also the right one. This isn't the overrun western Mediterranean loop (Barcelona, Mallorca, Marseille) that every entry-level cruise runs. The Adriatic and Ionian coasts are quieter, and combining Greece, Montenegro, Croatia, and Italy in a single trip is hard to match independently at this price point.

Kotor in particular is worth calling out. The bay entrance is narrow and dramatic — you sail through something that looks like a fjord before the medieval walls appear. It's the kind of thing that doesn't land the same way on a bus tour from Split.

At $1,639 retail this would be fair pricing for this route. At $899, it's genuinely good.

## The catch

The base fare doesn't include drinks, tips, or Wi-Fi. Princess's "Princess Plus" package bundles all three for an additional $65 per person per night — $455 extra for the full week, bringing your real per-person cost to $1,354 if you add it. That's still below the $1,639 retail baseline, but you're not traveling at $899 unless you want to track every drink you order.

Without Princess Plus, gratuities alone typically run $18–$20 per person per day (around $133–$140 for the week), and a drink package separately runs $70–$100 per person per day. The math favors buying Princess Plus upfront.

Flights are not included. You'll need to get to the embarkation port — typically Civitavecchia, though some itineraries depart from Piraeus (Athens). Round-trip economy from US hubs to Rome currently runs $650–$900; fold that in before comparing this to an all-inclusive.

One timing note: **check cancellation terms before you book.** Spring 2026 sailings (May–June) are close, and you may already be inside a partial or full penalty window. Fall sailings (September–November) booked now are almost certainly fully refundable — 4+ months of runway gives you time to evaluate before the penalties kick in.
